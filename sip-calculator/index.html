How does SIP Calculator work?

A SIP calculator uses the compound interest formula to project the future value of your systematic investments. It takes into account your monthly investment amount, the expected rate of return, and the investment duration.

Formula: FV = P × ({[1 + i]^n – 1} / i) × (1 + i)

Where: FV = Future Value, P = Monthly Investment, n = Total months, i = Periodic rate of interest

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good SIP amount to start with?

You can start with as low as ₹500 per month. The key is consistency. A small amount invested regularly over a long period can create significant wealth due to compounding.

Are SIP returns taxable?

Yes, returns are subject to capital gains tax. Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG) above ₹1 Lakh in a financial year are taxed at 10% without indexation. Short Term Capital Gains (STCG) are taxed at 15%.

SIP vs Lumpsum Investment

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

  • Invest small amounts regularly (e.g., ₹500/month).
  • Benefit from Rupee Cost Averaging (buy more units when market is low).
  • Disciplined investing habit.
  • Best for salaried individuals.

Lumpsum (One-time)

  • Invest a large amount at once.
  • Best when market valuations are low.
  • No rupee cost averaging benefit.
  • Suitable for bonus/windfall income deployment.
