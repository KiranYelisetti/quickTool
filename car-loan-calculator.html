Understanding Car Loans in India

What is a Car Loan?

A car loan (also called auto loan or vehicle loan) is a secured loan provided by banks and NBFCs to help you purchase a new or used car. You repay the loan through fixed monthly installments (EMIs) over 1-7 years. Banks typically finance 80-90% of the on-road price for new cars and 65-75% for used cars.

Smart Car Loan Strategies

Higher Down Payment

Pay 25-30% upfront instead of minimum 10-15%. Reduces EMI burden and saves ₹50K-1L in interest for a ₹10L car!

Shorter Tenure

Choose 3-4 years over 6-7 years. Cars depreciate fast - don't pay for a car longer than it's worth!

Compare Rates

1% difference on ₹5L loan = ₹15,000 extra over 5 years. Shop around for best rates!

Improve Credit Score

750+ score gets 1-2% lower rates. Pay credit card bills on time for 6 months before applying!

Important Things to Remember

  • Cars are depreciating assets - They lose 15-20% value in first year, 50% in 3 years
  • Insurance renewal costs - Budget ₹15K-20K annually after 1st year
  • Maintenance costs increase - After warranty (3-5 years), service costs rise significantly
  • Fuel + maintenance - Budget ₹3K-8K monthly depending on usage
  • Avoid 7-year loans - You'll be underwater (owe more than car's worth) after 4 years
  • Rule of thumb - Car loan EMI should not exceed 15% of monthly income
